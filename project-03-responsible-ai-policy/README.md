# Project 3 — Responsible AI Policy & Operating Model

## Scenario
NorthStar Financial Services — EU fintech, 1,400 employees, retail banking, lending, insurance, and compliance operations.

## Problem
Projects 1 and 2 identified material regulatory exposure — three High Risk AI systems with insufficient governance controls, two in 
production without conformity assessments, and no organisation-wide AI governance framework. Governance failures were structural, not technical.

## What I Built
Four professional governance documents constituting NorthStar's complete Responsible AI Framework:

1. Responsible AI Policy (NS-POL-AI-001)
   The master governance document — 14 sections covering scope, regulatory basis, risk classification, AI principles, governance 
   obligations by risk tier, governance structure, deployment lifecycle, incident management, vendor requirements, and remediation priorities.

2. AI Principles Statement (NS-PRIN-AI-001) Board-level document stating NorthStar's six AI principles — Fairness, Transparency, Human 
   Oversight, Accountability, Privacy, Robustness. Each principle maps to specific EU AI Act articles and operational controls.

3. AI Governance Operating Model (NS-OM-AI-001) Operational blueprint for AI governance at NorthStar — governance layers, AI Risk Committee 
   specification, system owner responsibilities, new system onboarding process, escalation framework, annual review cycle, and training 
   requirements.

4. Policy Implementation Roadmap (NS-ROAD-AI-001) 90-day remediation programme with 18 actions across 3 phases. Each action has a named owner, 
   deadline, EU AI Act basis, and success criteria.

## Key Decisions Made

Every policy clause directly responds to a finding from Projects 1 and 2:

- TalentMatch AI human override mandate responds to 2-year production gap with zero conformity assessment
- Bias audit requirement responds to zero demographic monitoring across High Risk systems
- AI Risk Committee responds to Board having zero visibility of AI risk classification
- Vendor contract requirements respond to FraudTech GmbH contract missing EU AI Act clauses
- Go-live gate process responds to DocIntel Underwriting approaching production with incomplete governance

## Frameworks Applied
- EU AI Act — Articles 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 28, 43, 52, 71, 73 and Annex III
- NIST AI RMF 1.0 — GOVERN function in full
- GDPR — Articles 5, 6, 22
